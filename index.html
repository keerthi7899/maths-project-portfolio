1. Introduction to Probability

Probability is mathematical study of uncertainty. It allows us to predict the likelihood of future events based on sample spaces and outcomes.

2. Fundamental Rules

Addition Rule

P(A ∪ B) = P(A) + P(B) - P(A ∩ B)

Used to find the probability that either Event A or Event B (or both) occurs.

Bayes' Theorem

Bayes' Theorem finds the posterior probability of an event based on prior conditions.

[Image of Bayes' theorem formula for conditional probability]
P(A|B) = [P(B|A) P(A)] / P(B)
Problem: In a group, 40% are engineers. If a test is 90% accurate for engineers and 20% "false positive" for others, find the probability a person is an engineer given they passed the test.

Solution using Bayes' Rule:
P(E) = 0.4, P(E') = 0.6
P(Pass|E) = 0.9, P(Pass|E') = 0.2
Total P(Pass) = (0.4 * 0.9) + (0.6 * 0.2) = 0.36 + 0.12 = 0.48
P(E|Pass) = (0.36 / 0.48) = 0.75

3. Step-by-Step Solved Problems

Problem 1: Finding the Constant 'k' and Mean

Question: A discrete random variable X has the probability distribution:
X = {0, 1, 2, 3} with P(X) = {0.1, k, 2k, 0.3}. Find 'k' and the Mean E[X].

Step 1: Use the property of Total Probability The sum of all probabilities in a distribution must equal 1.
Σ P(X) = 1
0.1 + k + 2k + 0.3 = 1
0.4 + 3k = 1
3k = 0.6 → k = 0.2

Step 2: Update the distribution P(X=1) = 0.2, P(X=2) = 0.4

Step 3: Calculate the Mean (Expected Value) E[X] = Σ [x * P(x)]
E[X] = (0 * 0.1) + (1 * 0.2) + (2 * 0.4) + (3 * 0.3)
E[X] = 0 + 0.2 + 0.8 + 0.9
Mean E[X] = 1.9

Problem 2: Binomial Distribution (Success/Failure)

Question: The probability that a component is defective is 0.1. If 5 components are tested, what is the probability that exactly 2 are defective?

Step 1: Identify Parameters n = 5 (trials), p = 0.1 (success/defective), q = 0.9 (failure), x = 2

Step 2: Apply Binomial Formula P(X=x) = nCx * px * qn-x
P(X=2) = 5C2 * (0.1)2 * (0.9)5-2

Step 3: Calculate Values 5C2 = 10
P(X=2) = 10 * 0.01 * (0.9)3
P(X=2) = 10 * 0.01 * 0.729
P(X=2) = 0.0729

Problem 3: Normal Distribution (Z-Score)

Question: The mean weight of a product is 50g with a standard deviation of 2g. Find the probability that a product weighs more than 53g.

Step 1: Identify Parameters μ = 50, σ = 2, X = 53

Step 2: Standardize to Z-score Z = (X - μ) / σ
Z = (53 - 50) / 2 = 3 / 2 = 1.5

Step 3: Find Area using Standard Normal Table We need P(X > 53), which is equivalent to P(Z > 1.5).
From tables, Area from 0 to 1.5 = 0.4332
P(Z > 1.5) = 0.5 - 0.4332
Probability = 0.0668 (6.68%)

Problem 4: Poisson Distribution (Rate per Interval)

Question: On average, 2 customers arrive at a counter every minute. Find the probability that exactly 3 customers arrive in a given minute.

Step 1: Identify Parameter (λ) λ (Average rate) = 2

Step 2: Apply Poisson Formula P(X=x) = (e-λ * λx) / x!
P(X=3) = (e-2 * 23) / 3!

Step 3: Calculate e-2 ≈ 0.1353
P(X=3) = (0.1353 * 8) / 6
P(X=3) = 1.0824 / 6
P(X=3) = 0.1804

Chapter 4: Statistical Inference

Hypothesis Testing, Confidence Intervals, and Sample Size Determination

1. Basic Terminologies of Hypothesis Testing

Statistical inference uses sample data to make conclusions about a whole population. Hypothesis testing is a formal procedure for investigating our ideas about the world using statistics.

  • Null Hypothesis (H0): The claim of "no effect" or "no difference." It is assumed true until evidence suggests otherwise.
  • Alternative Hypothesis (H1): The claim we are trying to find evidence for.
  • Level of Significance (α): The probability of rejecting the null hypothesis when it is actually true (Type I Error). Commonly 0.05 or 0.01.
  • P-value: The probability of obtaining results at least as extreme as the observed results, assuming H0 is true.
  • Critical Value: The threshold value that defines the rejection region.

2. Confidence Intervals (CI)

A confidence interval provides a range of values within which the true population parameter is likely to fall.

CI = Sample Mean ± (Critical Value * Standard Error)

For Means: x̄ ± Zα/2 * (σ / √n)

3. Sample Size Determination

To ensure a study has enough power to detect an effect, we calculate the minimum sample size (n) required based on a desired margin of error (E).

n = (Zα/2 * σ / E)2

4. Step-by-Step Solved Problems

Problem 1: Hypothesis Test for Single Mean (Large Sample)

Question: A manufacturer claims that the mean life of a battery is 500 days. A sample of 100 batteries shows a mean life of 490 days with a standard deviation of 40 days. Test the claim at 5% significance level.

Step 1: State the Hypotheses H0: μ = 500 (Claim is true)
H1: μ ≠ 500 (Two-tailed test)

Step 2: Identify Parameters n = 100, x̄ = 490, σ = 40, α = 0.05

Step 3: Calculate Test Statistic (Z) Z = (x̄ - μ) / (σ / √n)
Z = (490 - 500) / (40 / √100)
Z = -10 / 4 = -2.5

Step 4: Determine Critical Value For α = 0.05 (Two-tailed), Zcritical = ±1.96.

Step 5: Conclusion Since | -2.5 | > 1.96, the test statistic falls in the rejection region.
Decision: Reject H0. There is sufficient evidence to say the mean life is not 500 days.

Problem 2: Test for Difference of Means

Question: Two groups of students were taught using different methods.
Group A: n1=50, x̄1=75, s1=8
Group B: n2=60, x̄2=72, s2=10
Is there a significant difference between the two methods at 1% significance?

Step 1: State Hypotheses H0: μ1 = μ2
H1: μ1 ≠ μ2

Step 2: Calculate Standard Error (SE) SE = √[(s12/n1) + (s22/n2)]
SE = √[(64/50) + (100/60)] = √[1.28 + 1.66] = 1.714

Step 3: Calculate Z-test Statistic Z = (x̄1 - x̄2) / SE
Z = (75 - 72) / 1.714 = 1.75

Step 4: Conclusion For α = 0.01, Zcritical = ±2.58.
Since 1.75 < 2.58, we Fail to Reject H0.
Result: No significant difference between methods.

Problem 3: Hypothesis Test for Proportions

Question: A politician claims that 60% of voters support him. In a sample of 200 voters, 108 support him. Test the claim at α=0.05.

Step 1: State Hypotheses H0: P = 0.60
H1: P ≠ 0.60

Step 2: Calculate Sample Proportion (p̂) p̂ = 108 / 200 = 0.54

Step 3: Calculate Z-test Statistic Z = (p̂ - P) / √[P(1-P)/n]
Z = (0.54 - 0.60) / √[(0.6 * 0.4) / 200]
Z = -0.06 / 0.0346 = -1.73

Step 4: Conclusion Critical Z = ±1.96.
Since |-1.73| < 1.96, we Fail to Reject H0.
Result: The claim that 60% support him stands.

Problem 4: Constructing a Confidence Interval

Question: A sample of 64 measurements has a mean of 20 and a standard deviation of 4. Find the 95% confidence interval for the population mean.

Step 1: Identify Values n=64, x̄=20, σ=4, Z0.05/2 = 1.96

Step 2: Calculate Margin of Error (E) E = 1.96 * (4 / √64) = 1.96 * 0.5 = 0.98

Step 3: Determine Interval Lower Limit = 20 - 0.98 = 19.02
Upper Limit = 20 + 0.98 = 20.98

95% CI: [19.02, 20.98]
